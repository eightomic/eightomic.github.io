About

I'm William Stafford Parsons, the sole creator of Eightomic and long-time software engineer turned solo game developer.

Video games are a perfect blend of art and science, expressive and functional, with one either an asset or a bottleneck to the other.

Creating them opens a rare opportunity to craft experiences to enhance a few moments of someoneâ€™s life.

Eightomic brings minimal-style video games with console-quality, unusual gameplay to modern desktop web browsers using pure CSS, HTML and JavaScript.

Each game uses only 8 keys on the keyboard, specifically the arrow keys and w a s d.

They're fun for a long period of time with intuitive controls and super-polished gameplay to preserve the feeling of wanting to insert a quarter into an arcade machine just to experience 5 minutes of a video game.

Eightomic respects that you probably have a busy life outside of gaming and may already have hundreds of other games to grind, so each game is built without HUDs, XP grinding, achievement hunting, in-game currency purchases, objectives, loot boxes or malicious downloads.

As a result, Eightomic is able to focus on creating fun games where the arcade-style, high-quality, meditative gameplay experience is the only reward system.

If I don't have fun playing the games I create, I don't expect others to enjoy playing them. Therefore, Eightomic games are the only games that I play.

Furthermore, Eightomic conducts open computer science research occasionally in the area of optimizing "close-to-the-machine" algorithms that have the most potential to speed up popular game engines when applied correctly, although the sustainability and user experience impact spans far beyond.

My dream is to eventually earn a living by doing exactly what I'm doing on Eightomic because I'd do it regardless.
